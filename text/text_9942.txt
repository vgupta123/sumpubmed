BACKGROUND
cav is a small non-enveloped, single-stranded, circular dna virus and was first isolated in japan  <cit> . this virus belongs to the genus gyrovirus of the circoviridae family and causes a severe immunosuppressive syndrome and anemia in chickens  <cit> . it is a ubiquitous pathogen of chickens and has a worldwide distribution. according to epidemiological studies, it has been shown that almost all newly hatched chicks are susceptible to cav as a clinical syndrome, but not mature chickens  <cit> . normally, young chickens, generally fewer than  <dig> weeks of age, are very susceptible to this virus through vertical transmission via hatching eggs  <cit> . the virus typically induces aplasia of the bone marrow and damage to lymphoid tissue, which causes anemia and acute immunodeficiency syndrome  <cit> . up to the present, a large number of isolates, including strains from australia, bangladesh, brazil, china, germany, malaysia, nigeria, slovenia, taiwan and usa, have been reported and have had full or partial sequences published  <cit> .

the dna genome of cav is about  <dig>  kb in size  <cit>  and there are three orfs present on the negative sense genome. at least three proteins are produced from a single polycistronic  <dig>  kb mrna that is reliable produced as a single molecule and contains a promoter, tata-box, and poly  signal  <cit> . the three translated proteins are called vp <dig>  vp <dig> and vp <dig>  vp <dig> is a  <dig> kda protein that is the structure protein involved in assembly of the viral caspid  <cit> . vp <dig> is a  <dig> kda protein that contains a dual-specificity phosphatase  activity and some apoptotic activity  <cit> . however, vp2's apoptotic activity is much weaker than that of vp <dig>  vp <dig> is a  <dig> kda protein, also named apoptin, which induces apoptosis in infected chicken cells and human tumor cell lines  <cit> . during virus infection, vp <dig> and vp <dig> are detected very early, namely  <dig> h post infection, while vp <dig> is detected only after  <dig> h post infection  <cit> . some additional proteins have been reported to be translated after further splicing of the mrna, but their biological functions have not been elucidated  <cit> .

as mention above, vp <dig> is a dsp enzyme  <cit> . the key catalytic residues of active site have been identified to be serine, threonine, and tyrosine. the cysteine residues, respectively, are located at positions  <dig> and  <dig> in the catalytic motif of vp <dig>  furthermore, mutation of these residues to serine results in reduced virus replication efficiency in the cell  <cit> . this effect indicates that the phosphatase activity of vp <dig> is required for virus replication. it has been reported that co-expression of vp <dig> and vp <dig> allows neutralizing antibodies to be raised  <cit>  and it has been suggested that vp <dig> is a scaffold protein  <cit>  that corrects the conformation of vp <dig>  therefore, it is expected that vp <dig> is a multifunctional protein with roles in virus infection, assembly and replication. vp <dig> possess a putative nls and the protein is known to accumulate to a large extent in the nucleus of infected chicken cells  <cit> . a recent study has shown that vp <dig>  when fused to gfp, shows nuclear localization and this result indicates that the nls of vp <dig> is also functional in plants  <cit> .

until now, the specific mechanism for the cellular localization of vp <dig> is not well understood. in this study, we first use bioinformatics to analysis the amino acid sequence of various different isolates of vp <dig>  and were able to predict and examine for the presence of putative nls and nes motifs, which have never been characterized previously. we generated gfp fused to various versions of vp <dig> created by truncation, site directed mutagenesis, and multiple site directed mutagenesis in order to confirm the locations of these putative nls and nes sequences. leptomycin b  can be used to identify the presence of a nes motif in a protein because it inhibits the crm <dig> pathway and such a result has been found for vp <dig> of cav. using lmb, our results suggest that vp <dig> does not contain a functional nes and also that vp <dig> is crm <dig> independent. additionally, using a co-immunoprecipitation assay, we also found that vp <dig> associates with mcm <dig> and that this interaction does not require dsp activity.

in previous studies, it has been shown by indirect immunoperoxidase staining that vp <dig> is localized within the nuclei of mdcc-msb  <dig> cells infected with cav  <cit> . in  <dig>  lacorte et al. reported that transient expression of gfp-vp <dig> could be observed throughout the nucleoplasm in plant cells  <cit> . in the present study, we constructed expression plasmids of vp2-gfp in order to identify the nuclear localizing characteristics in cells. after transient transfection, the bright fluorescence of vp2-gfp was observed mostly in the nucleus of both hela and cho cells. in contrast, when gfp alone was introduced, not only the nucleus but also the cytoplasm was fluorescent. this indicates that a functional nls was present in vp <dig> as suggested by previously .

discussion
cav is an important avian pathogen worldwide and causes major economic damage throughout the poultry industry. three major proteins are encoded by this virus, namely the capsid protein vp <dig>  the nonstructural protein vp <dig>  and the apoptin vp <dig>  however, up to the present, the exactly functions of these three proteins in cell are still poorly understood. in previous studies, the expression of vp <dig> was found to be nuclear in plant cells  <cit> . this result is similar to that of the present study, where, vp <dig> was found to accumulate in the nucleus of both hela and cho cells. our results, both by bioinformatics and site-directed mutagenesis, support the hypothesis that vp <dig> contain a nls and named as nsl <dig>  nls <dig> was shown to be a functional nls that allowed transfer of vp <dig> into nucleus. this is similar to vp <dig>  which has two functional nls motifs that are also used for nuclear localization. the single vp <dig> nls motif spans amino acid residues  <dig> to  <dig>  within this motif, we also found that two amino acids with positive charges, 133r and  <dig> k were important to allowing vp <dig> protein to shuttle from the cytoplasm to the nucleus. to the best of our knowledge, this is the first report identifying a nls motif in the vp <dig> of cav.

subsequent sequence analysis showed that vp <dig> might contain a putative crm1-mediated nes motif that stretched from amino acid  <dig> to amino acid  <dig>  however the scores for this motif was below the program threshold. in order to confirm that a nes motif really existed in vp <dig>  we used fusion protein constructs to investigate nuclear transportation in the presence of lmb assay . a control protein, namely vp <dig>  also named apoptin, which contains a classical leucine-rich nes motif and has been previously described as sensitive to lmb treatment was included in the study  <cit> . our results confirmed that the cytoplasm and nuclear distribution of the various truncated vp <dig> mutants was not obviously changed in pattern after treatment with lmb. this supports two possibilities; firstly, that the nuclear export pathway  of vp <dig> might not exist and/or, secondly, that a distinct and crm <dig> dependent pathway was adopted by vp <dig> for nuclear export.

vp <dig> has been shown to contain dual phosphatase activity and this activity is required for cav replication  <cit> . however, in this context, very little is known about the role this protein plays in the regulation of viral dna replication. when cells are infected with cav, vp <dig> is expressed and accumulates, reaching a detectable level at  <dig> h post-infection  <cit> . in contrast, vp <dig> has been found to accumulate to a higher level at  <dig> h post-infection. amino acids sequence analysis of cav vp <dig> was revealed that the n-terminal region show similarity to protamines  <cit> , this supports the hypothesis that vp <dig> has dna-binding functionality  <cit> . the n and c-terminal domains of vp <dig> separately bind to dna and indicating the presence of multiple independent binding sites  <cit> . however, vp <dig> protein has no obvious dna binding characteristics. in this study, vp <dig> of cav is shown to be localized to the nucleus in two cell lines  and also to bind to chromatin . the latter finding was confirmed by examining the nuclease-resistant fraction and by treating with high salt. vp <dig> is similar to mcm <dig>  a component of various mcm2- <dig> complexes, and other nuclease-resistant chromatin bound proteins such as cdc <dig>  <cit> , minichromosome maintenance proteins   <cit> , and pcna  <cit> , all of which have chromatin-bound characteristics. this suggests that the early expression of vp <dig> might involve dna replication with vp <dig> interacting with the prereplication complex . the association of vp <dig> with mcm <dig>  may facilitate the dna replication of cav. however, it is still unknown whether vp <dig> is able to bind to dna directly and this will need further investigation. moreover, vp2's association with mcm <dig> does not require dual phosphatase activity  and therefore, the relevance of vp2's dsp activity to the live cycle of cav also needs further investigation.

rep, a viral protein with replicase activity involved in regulating rolling-circle replication , has been found in most circovirus members of the family circoviridae  <cit> . cav seems to lack a rep protein. however, some studies have proposed that the c-terminus of cav vp <dig> contains a highly conserved rcr motif and that this may play a role in regulating the rcr reaction  <cit> . this rcr-regulating function of vp <dig> seems to be very similar to that of the rep protein. therefore, vp <dig> may interact with vp <dig> to form a nucleoprotein complex and, in addition, the genomic dna of the virus might also be coupled with vp <dig> and vp <dig> in order to regulate the rcr reaction during the early stages of infection. however, these hypotheses need to be investigated in terms of how the mcms interact with the vp <dig> of cav.

CONCLUSIONS
we have demonstrated that the vp <dig> of cav contains a functional nls motif that spans amino acids  <dig> to  <dig> of the protein. in addition to having a nls, vp <dig> is also a chromatin binding protein similar to members of the mcm2- <dig> complex. moreover, vp <dig> associates with mcm <dig> in cells based on co-immunoprecipitation analysis. taken together, these findings suggest that vp <dig> may be part of a dna pre-replication complex.
