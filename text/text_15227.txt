BACKGROUND
the atp-binding cassette  transporters form one of the largest family of membrane proteins  <cit> . with  <dig> members in total, the abc transporter family from animals was first identified in the human genome  <cit> . ranging from  <dig> to ~ <dig> members, the abc transporter family is present in all organisms  <cit> . based on sequence similarity of the atp-binding sites, the  <dig> human abc transporters can be classified into seven subfamilies . the eighth subfamily  was defined following the analysis of the genome of the fruitfly, drosophila melanogaster   <cit> . in addition to the fruitfly  <cit> , the abc transporters have been previously analyzed in another insect species, the mosquito, anopheles gambiae , at the genome-wide level  <cit> .

the abc transporters share highly conserved domains known as nucleotide binding domains . each nbd contains three characteristic motifs, including walker a box, walker b box, and abc signature c which links the two walker boxes  <cit> . nbd binds and hydrolyses atp and provides energy to transport molecules against concentration gradients. in addition to nbd, a eukaryotic abc transporter usually consists of one or two transmembrane domains . the abc transporters which have two nbds and two tmds are called full transporters, whereas those with one nbd and one tmd are called half transporters, which often constitute a functional unit by forming a homo- or heterodimer  <cit> .

according to their functions, the abc proteins can be classified as importers, exporters and non-transport proteins  <cit> . importers and exporters are responsible for transport of a wide variety of substances, whereas the third class of abc proteins are apparently not involved in molecule transport but in cellular processes such as dna repair, translation or regulation of gene expression  <cit> . in human, known functions of abc transporters include cholesterol and lipid transport, multidrug resistance, antigen presentation, mitochondrial iron homeostasis, and the atp-dependent regulation of ion channels. mutations in abc genes have been associated with a range of disorders, including cystic fibrosis, hypercholesterolemia and diabetes  <cit> . in insects, it has been shown that abc transporters have roles in uric acid metabolism, development and possibly in insecticide resistance  <cit> . due to the importance of abc transporters, several members of abc transporters have been extensively studied in several model insects, including the fruitfly and the silkworm, bombyx mori . one of the best studied insect abc transporters is white, which is a typical abcg transporter involved in pigment transport in insect eyes  <cit> .

in a microarray study published previously  <cit> , we detected multiple silkworm abc genes exhibiting possible regulation by the molting hormone, 20-hydroxyecdysone . to this end, we decided to identify and characterize the silkworm abc transporters at the genome-wide level. very recently, three silkworm abc transporter subfamilies, including abcb, abcc and abcg, were analyzed regarding to their possible xenobiotic resistance  <cit> . in this study, we have identified  <dig> putative silkworm abc genes in total and analyzed their phylogenetic relationships. moreover, we have investigated their temporal and spatial expression patterns using microarray and quantitative real-time pcr , with focus on the abcg subfamily.

abcd
the abcd transporters are located to the peroxisome at the subcellular level and involved in the import of fatty acids and/or fatty acyl-coas into this organelle  <cit> . each of the  <dig> insect species consists of  <dig> half transporters in the abcd subfamily. the insect abcd transporters are separated into two distinct groups by phylogenetic analysis , indicating that they are evolutionarily conserved in insects.

abce and abcf
the abce and abcf proteins lack tmd and each member contains a pair of linked nbds  <cit> . most eukaryotes have only one abce gene that is highly conserved during evolution . different from most abc transporters, their functions in cell biological processes are not related to transport. in human, abce <dig> was identified as an inhibitor of rnase l  <cit> , whereas in yeast, abce proteins play key roles in translation initiation  <cit> . the silkworm abce gene, bmabc <dig> which shows the highest expression level based on the est information, is preferentially expressed in testis, ovary and fat body. however, rna interference -mediated knockdown of bmabc <dig> has no obvious effects on rnase l expression  <cit> .

abcf proteins function in ribosome biogenesis, translational control, and mrna export, and are not involved in molecule transport  <cit> . the best-characterized abcf protein is the yeast gcn <dig> protein, which is involved in the initiation and control of translation. in addition, mutations in gcn <dig> gene reduce eif2Î± phosphorylation and thus translation in the ribosome  <cit> . later studies revealed that abcf <dig> physically interacts with eif <dig> and associates with the ribosome in an atp-dependent manner  <cit> . until now, the abcf subfamily has not been studied in insects. the same as human and most of other organisms, each of the  <dig> insect species consists of  <dig> abcf proteins, which are separated into  <dig> distinct groups by phylogenetic analysis and genes in each group have high similarity . these data suggest that abce and abcf subfamilies are highly conserved during evolution in all living organisms.

tissue distribution analysis reveals some tissue-specific abc genes
in order to understand the possible physiological function and hormonal regulation of the silkworm abc genes in the future, we here analyzed the expression patterns of all the silkworm abc genes by microarray data. with the exception of bmabc <dig>  all other abc genes have corresponding probes in the oligonucleotide chip  <cit> . we first investigated the spatial expression patterns of the silkworm abc genes on day  <dig> of fifth larval instar using the microarray data of tissue-specific expression  <cit> . the analysis revealed that the expression of  <dig> abc genes can be detected at least in one of the selected tissues, including testis, ovary, head, integument, malpighian tubule, fat body, haemocyte, anterior and middle silk gland, and posterior silk gland . significantly, a large number of abc genes were highly expressed in the testis compared to other tissues. it is known that more than one thousand genes are expressed specifically in the testis  <cit> . however, whether all these testis-specific abc transporters are functionally important in this male organ is questionable and worthy of further investigation.

some tissue-specific abc genes were verified by qrt-pcr in nine tissues, including fat body, midgut, testis and ovary, wing disc, trachea, brain, malpighian tubule, haemocyte, prothoracic gland, and silk gland, on day  <dig> and  <dig> of fifth larval instar and day  <dig> of prepupae. besides the testis, the malpighian tubule is the second tissue enriched with abc gene expressions. the malpighian tubule serves as the excretory and osmoregulatory organs for insects, in which the urine is produced and transported to the hindgut for the selective absorption of water and ions. thus, the malpighian tubule plays an important role in excretion and it is also involved in xenobiotic detoxification  <cit> . bmabc <dig>  an abcg gene, was exclusively and abundantly expressed in the malpighian tubule, detected by both microarray  and qrt-pcr , suggesting that bmabc <dig> might play an important role in the malpighian tubule. bmabc <dig> could serve as a malpighian tubule-specific molecule marker for future studies of gene function and regulation.

it has been reported that the tmd of abcg <dig> binds steroids and the binding mediates modulation of abcg <dig> activity  <cit> . however, little is known about whether and how abcg genes are transcriptionally regulated by steroid hormones in mammals. since gene duplication is very evident in the abcg subfamily in insects but not in human, the similarity of abcg transporters in both physiological function and hormonal regulation is of great interest. we suppose that transcriptional regulation of abcg genes by the steroid hormone 20e in the silkworm holds promising values for further investigation.

CONCLUSIONS
this study provides a genome-wide analysis of the abc transporters from the silkworm and a comparative analysis of the abc family from  <dig> insect species. a large number of silkworm abc genes are highly expressed in the testis and the malpighian tubule. two abcg genes, bmabc <dig> and bmabc <dig>  are exclusively expressed in the malpighian tubule and the midgut, respectively. moreover,  <dig> abcg genes are preferentially expressed in the midgut and their transcriptional levels are up-regulated by 20e during molting and pupation. the descriptive study of silkworm abcg genes lays a foundation for future analysis of their physiological function and hormonal regulation, using both the rnai approach  <cit>  and the binary gal4/uas transgenic system  <cit> .
